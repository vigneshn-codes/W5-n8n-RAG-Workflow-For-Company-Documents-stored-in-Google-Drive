## POST 1 — Technical Engineering Audience

---

🔧 Built a production-ready RAG pipeline on n8n — here's the full architecture breakdown.

I recently shipped a self-hosted Retrieval-Augmented Generation workflow that auto-indexes company documents from Google Drive and serves answers through a LangChain AI Agent. Here's how it works under the hood:

**📥 Ingestion Pipeline**
→ Dual Google Drive triggers (`fileCreated` + `fileUpdated`) poll a watched folder every minute
→ New/updated files are downloaded as binary and passed to a **Default Data Loader**
→ A **Recursive Character Text Splitter** (100-token overlap) ensures context continuity across chunk boundaries
→ **OpenAI Embeddings** (`text-embedding-ada-002`) convert chunks to dense vectors
→ All vectors are upserted into a shared **in-memory vector store** under a consistent key

**💬 Query Pipeline**
→ A **Chat Trigger** fires on incoming employee questions
→ A **LangChain AI Agent** (GPT-4o-mini) equipped with a `company_documents_tool` performs semantic retrieval
→ **Window Buffer Memory** maintains multi-turn conversation state across the session
→ Retrieved context is synthesized and returned through an **Edit Fields** formatter node

**Technical decisions worth noting:**
- Shared `vector_store_key` between ingestion and retrieval stores ensures zero coordination overhead
- Separate LLM slots on both the Agent and Vector Store Tool nodes allow independent model swapping
- The fallback response ("I cannot find the answer...") is baked into the system prompt — no hallucination drift
- The in-memory store is intentionally simple for demo purposes; Pinecone/Qdrant drop-ins are straightforward

**What I'd change for production:**
- Replace polling triggers with Drive webhooks for real-time ingestion
- Add upsert logic with file hash checks to prevent duplicate embeddings on re-uploads
- Swap the Simple Vector Store for Pinecone with namespace-based tenant isolation

The entire workflow is ~18 nodes, zero custom code, and runs cleanly on a self-hosted n8n instance.

## POST 2 — General / Non-Technical Audience

---

🗂️ What if every employee could instantly find answers hidden in your company's documents?

Most teams have a growing pile of internal documents — HR policies, onboarding guides, product manuals, process documentation. The problem? Finding the right answer inside them takes forever.

I built an AI-powered assistant that solves this — and it's fully automated.

Here's how it works in plain English:

**Step 1: Watch your Google Drive**
The system keeps an eye on a specific Google Drive folder. Whenever a new document is added or an existing one is updated, it automatically picks it up — no manual steps needed.

**Step 2: Learn from the documents**
The document is read, broken into small pieces, and turned into a format that AI can search through intelligently. Think of it like building a smart index that understands meaning, not just keywords.

**Step 3: Answer employee questions**
Employees open a chat window and ask questions in plain language:
- *"What's our work-from-home policy?"*
- *"How do I submit an expense report?"*
- *"What are the steps for onboarding a new vendor?"*

The AI searches the documents, finds the most relevant sections, and gives a clear, concise answer — instantly.

If the answer isn't in the documents, it says so honestly. No guessing.

**Why this matters:**
✅ Saves hours of searching through folders
✅ Works 24/7, no human support needed
✅ Answers stay accurate because they're grounded in your actual documents
✅ Documents stay in your own Google Drive — nothing leaves your environment

No complicated software. No IT overhaul. Just smarter access to knowledge your company already has.

This is what automation looks like when it actually solves a real problem.
